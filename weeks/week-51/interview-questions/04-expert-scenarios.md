## Q101: Panel Technical Leadership Simulation

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Difficulty** | Expert |
| **Category** | STAR Behavioral |
| **Frequency** | Very Common |
| **Type** | Scenario |

### Question

In a panel interview, how defend your technical leadership decisions when two interviewers challenge different aspects?

### Short Answer (30 seconds)

Acknowledge each concern, use evidence not ego, prioritize concerns by risk, offer phased approach. Stay calm — collaboration not combat.

### Detailed Answer

**Simulation scenario:**
Interviewer A: 'Your standard slowed teams.'
Interviewer B: 'Why not wait for platform team?'

**Response framework:**
1. Validate A — 'Velocity concern was real in month 1'
2. Evidence — Pilot showed 2-week slowdown, 6-month defect reduction
3. Address B — Platform backlog 9 months; business couldn't wait
4. Synthesis — Phased enforcement: new services first, legacy exempt 2 quarters
5. Invite — 'Happy to go deeper on metrics or rollout'

**Expert signal:** Disagree with interviewer respectfully using data; don't flip-flop between answers.

### Architecture Perspective

Panel defense simulates staff architect daily — multiple stakeholders, one table.

### Follow-up Questions

1. **Contradictory panel? — Bridge: 'Both concerns shaped phased rollout.'**
2. **Freeze under dual challenge? — Practice with two mock interviewers.**

### Common Mistakes in Interviews

- Argue with interviewer
- Flip position without rationale
- Dismiss velocity concern entirely

## Q102: Expert Failure Panel Challenge

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Difficulty** | Expert |
| **Category** | STAR Behavioral |
| **Frequency** | Very Common |
| **Type** | Scenario |

### Question

Panel asks: 'Your failure story sounds like the platform team's fault — clarify your accountability.' How respond?

### Short Answer (30 seconds)

Re-center your decision authority, acknowledge team execution context without blame, restate lesson you own. Diplomatic accountability.

### Detailed Answer

**Response script:**
'Thank you for pushing on that — I owned the architecture recommendation and migration sequencing. Platform team executed per design; the flaw was my capacity model assumption. I've since added load validation gate before any cutover — that gate is mine to enforce as architect.'

**Avoid:**
- Throwing platform under bus
- Absorbing others' negligence if untrue — nuance matters
- Arguing with panel about their interpretation

**Expert move:** Offer to walk through decision record (ADR) you wrote — shows documentation discipline.

### Architecture Perspective

Accountability challenge tests emotional intelligence under adversarial panel tone.

### Follow-up Questions

1. **Panel playing devil's advocate? — Assume good faith; don't get defensive.**
2. **Legal sensitivity? — Pre-agree story with counsel if real incident.**

### Common Mistakes in Interviews

- Blame another team in panel
- Deny accountability entirely
- Argue with interviewer about facts

## Q103: Expert Mentoring Ethics Scenario

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Difficulty** | Expert |
| **Category** | STAR Behavioral |
| **Frequency** | Very Common |
| **Type** | Scenario |

### Question

Expert scenario: Mentee asks you to exaggerate their contribution in promotion packet. How handle in interview STAR format?

### Short Answer (30 seconds)

Integrity STAR: refuse exaggeration, help articulate real impact with evidence, escalate to manager if pressure continues. Show principled mentorship.

### Detailed Answer

**STAR answer:**
- **S:** Mentee wanted inflated bullet in architect promotion case
- **T:** Mentor accountable for honest advocacy
- **A:** Reviewed actual contributions; co-wrote accurate CAR bullets; explained promotion committee detects inflation; offered to sponsor real scope expansion project
- **R:** Mentee promoted next cycle with honest packet; trust preserved

**Principles:** Advocate strongly with truth; teach self-advocacy without fabrication.

### Architecture Perspective

Ethics mentoring scenario tests integrity — common at senior levels.

### Follow-up Questions

1. **Mentee left angry? — OK if principled stance explained.**
2. **Manager pressured inflation? — Escalation story if experienced.**

### Common Mistakes in Interviews

- Agreed to exaggerate to help mentee
- Publicly shamed mentee
- No alternative path offered
