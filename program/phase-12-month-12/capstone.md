# Month 12 Capstone — Graduation Portfolio + Panel Mock

> **Phase 12** | Complete after weeks in this month.

## Brief

Portfolio package + 3-round panel mock interview.

## Scenario

You have completed **52 weeks** of architect training and are preparing for **staff/principal architect** interviews and an internal graduation panel. Hiring managers expect a **portfolio** proving breadth (cloud, distributed systems, data, AI, DevOps) and depth (ADRs, diagrams, trade-off narratives). The graduation panel runs **three rounds**: system design (60 min), architecture review of your portfolio project (45 min), and behavioral/leadership (45 min). Constraints: portfolio must be self-contained (GitHub or static site), diagrams must render without proprietary tools, and STAR stories must map to real capstone work from months 1–11. Stakeholders are the program review board and your interview coach.

## Architecture Expectations

A passing graduation package must synthesize the full program:

- **Portfolio site or repo** — index page linking 5+ architecture artifacts from prior capstones
- **Flagship diagram set** — at least 5 production-quality diagrams (C4, sequence, network, data, or AI pipeline)
- **ADR anthology** — 10 best ADRs curated from the year with one-page synthesis
- **Career narrative** — 2-page "architect journey" connecting learning to business outcomes
- **10 STAR stories** — Situation, Task, Action, Result; rehearsed aloud
- **Panel mock completion** — [3-round panel mock](../../interview-prep/mock-interviews/mock-06-panel-loop.md) scored ≥80%
- **Gap analysis** — honest self-assessment vs target role (staff vs principal)
- **90-day post-program plan** — certifications, open source, blog, or interview pipeline

## Deliverables

- [ ] **Portfolio index** — README or static site with navigation to all artifacts
- [ ] **5+ architecture diagrams** — curated from months 1–11, captioned and dated
- [ ] **ADR anthology** — 10 ADRs with 1-page synthesis of decision themes
- [ ] **Career narrative doc** — 2 pages, outcomes-focused
- [ ] **10 STAR stories** — table with story title, competency, capstone link
- [ ] **Panel mock session** — complete [mock-06-panel-loop](../../interview-prep/mock-interviews/mock-06-panel-loop.md) ≥80%
- [ ] **Mock debrief notes** — what went well, gaps, retry plan
- [ ] **Graduation presentation** — 20-slide or 20-minute talk outline
- [ ] **90-day post-program plan** — measurable goals
- [ ] **Week 52 final capstone alignment** — per [Week 52](../../weeks/week-52/README.md) checklist

## Evaluation Rubric

| Criteria | Points |
|----------|--------|
| Requirements clarity | 20 |
| Architecture quality | 30 |
| Trade-off documentation | 20 |
| Production realism | 15 |
| Presentation / ADRs | 15 |

**Requirements clarity (20 pts)**

- Portfolio scope and target role (staff/principal) stated
- Each artifact linked with context (which month, which problem)
- Panel mock requirements completed

**Architecture quality (30 pts)**

- Five diagrams represent diverse domains (not all Azure IaaS)
- Flagship project shows end-to-end ownership
- Diagrams meet professional presentation standard

**Trade-off documentation (20 pts)**

- ADR anthology shows evolution of thinking
- STAR stories include trade-offs and metrics
- Gap analysis is specific and actionable

**Production realism (15 pts)**

- Portfolio artifacts reflect production constraints from capstones
- Behavioral stories cite real stakeholder conflict
- Post-program plan is achievable in 90 days

**Presentation / ADRs (15 pts)**

- 20-minute graduation talk rehearsed
- Panel mock score ≥80% on provided rubric
- Peer or coach feedback incorporated

**Pass:** ≥ 70 points
